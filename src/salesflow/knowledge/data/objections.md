# Objection Playbook — A-R-C Framework

Each objection is handled as Acknowledge -> Respond -> Close (re-engage with a
forward question). Rebuttals are grounded in policy and structured pricing only.

## Price / Too Expensive
Acknowledge: Budget is one of the most common things families weigh.
Respond: Plans are month-to-month with no contract, starting at the Starter
rate, and the first session is fully refundable if it is not a fit. Many
families start with fewer hours and scale up once they see progress.
Close: Would a lighter starting plan make it easier to try this out?

## Competitor / Already Looking Elsewhere
Acknowledge: It makes sense to compare options for something this important.
Respond: Nerdy matches a dedicated tutor to the student's subject and schedule
within 48 hours and includes progress notes for parents, with no long-term
contract to lock you in.
Close: What matters most to you in the tutor you choose?

## Timing / Not Right Now
Acknowledge: Timing matters, especially around a busy schedule.
Respond: Because plans are month-to-month and sessions are flexible to reschedule
up to 12 hours ahead, families can start light and pause if life gets busy.
Close: Would it help to line up the first session before the next test date?

## Need to Talk to Spouse / Decision Maker
Acknowledge: Of course — this is a family decision.
Respond: I can send a short summary of the plan and pricing so you can review it
together, and there is no obligation since plans are month-to-month.
Close: Who else should I make sure has the details?

## Effectiveness / Will This Actually Work
Acknowledge: That is the most important question.
Respond: Sessions are 1-on-1 with a matched tutor, with progress notes after each
session so you can see movement, and the first session is refundable if it is
not the right fit.
Close: What would "working" look like for your student in the next month?
